2016 Sound Music Robot

A collaborative project, with artists Alex McKechnie, David Rogers and Adrian Newton improvising live electronic sound on the theme of world religions.

The musicians are joined on stage by Muffy, a piece of DIY software, designed to improvise sounds and images in response to live musicians.

August 18 2016, The West Bay, Bridport, Dorset. Force8 Festival.
An online prototype of MUFFY is available to play with. To interact, you will need to allow your browser to turn your microphone on when prompted. The pitch detection works for most computers but if you are unlucky with your sound card, MUFFY will get its pitch exactly one semitone out.

2015 Tokyo Syllables

In 2015 DIVAContemporary called for music on the theme of Japan for their 'Listen No.2' release.

I recalled that back in 1992 Alex Burrow's girlfriend went to Japan and came home with cassette tapes of Japanese radio for us. We made good use of them, and many improvisation sessions were still sat in the archive. I took this opportunity to go back through them and assembled this, in the spirit of early Barbed.

2010 Nosferatu, Live Soundtrack

On September 23 2010 b-side Festival projected the 1922 film Nosferatu, eine Symphonie des Grauens onto the side of a pub in the middle of Weymouth.

The soundtrack was improvised live by No Context, Alan Rogers, Alex McKechnie and Andy Cleeton.

2008 Storm Performance, Schiphorst AvantGarde Festival

The last Barbed performance was a collaboration with artist Hester Schofield at Faust's farmhouse festival in northern Germany. Hester provided an dynamic and atmospheric lighting environment to accompany a true story about an airman who fell from a plane through an electric storm. As he fell he saw lightning all around him as giant planes of light. He survived the fall.

This performance featured a lot of activity from a Soviet-era drum machine, the Formanta UDS, which regrettably did not survive the return journey.
